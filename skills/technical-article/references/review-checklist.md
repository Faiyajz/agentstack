# Review Checklist

## Reader Value

- The article has a clear reader and promise.
- The introduction explains why the topic matters.
- Concepts appear before they are used.
- The ending tells the reader what to do next.

## Technical Accuracy

- Claims are sourced or proven by the example.
- Version-sensitive behavior is current or labeled.
- Commands, paths, snippets, and outputs match the repo.
- Tradeoffs and limitations are explicit.

## Example Integration

- The example is complete and verified.
- Snippets match files in `code/`.
- The article explains how to run the example.
- File references are correct.

## Research

- Sources are recorded in `sources.json`.
- Official or primary sources support important claims.
- Outdated or conflicting sources are handled explicitly.

## Voice and Style

- Tone matches the requested author or platform.
- Signature phrases are natural, not forced.
- Forbidden phrases are absent.
- The writing is direct, concrete, and not padded.

## Final Quality

- Frontmatter is valid when required.
- Links work.
- Headings are useful.
- Code blocks have language tags.
- Known gaps are documented.
