# Behavioral Interview

Turn real experience into concise, credible interview stories with clear stakes, actions, and impact.

## Workflow

### 1. Gather

- Identify target role, level, company type, question type, and available experiences.
- Ask for facts when a story lacks stakes, ownership, conflict, result, or learning.
- Do not invent achievements.

### 2. Structure

Shape answers using:

- situation: context and why it mattered
- task: responsibility and constraints
- action: specific decisions and behavior
- result: measurable or observable outcome
- reflection: what changed afterward

### 3. Practice

- Ask one question at a time.
- Let the user answer before rewriting in mock mode.
- Evaluate clarity, credibility, seniority signal, ownership, humility, and relevance.

### 4. Improve

- Tighten vague claims into concrete evidence.
- Remove blame, filler, and overly technical detail.
- Prepare variants for different interviewers and time limits.

## Rules

- Never fabricate work history, metrics, titles, or company claims.
- Strong answers show judgment, not just success.
- For leadership roles, include people, alignment, tradeoffs, and follow-through.
- Keep answers concise enough to speak naturally.
