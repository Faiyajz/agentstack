# Interview Prep

Prepare targeted practice for the role, level, and interview format. Route focused sessions to specialized skills when possible.

## Workflow

### 1. Profile

- Identify target role, level, company type, timeline, interview rounds, strengths, weak areas, and preferred language or stack.
- Ask only when missing information would change the practice plan.

### 2. Plan Practice

Choose the right mix and use the specialized skill when the user wants a focused session:

- `coding-interview` for algorithms, data structures, debugging, and live coding
- `system-design-interview` for architecture, scale, APIs, data, reliability, and tradeoffs
- `behavioral-interview` for stories, leadership, conflict, ownership, and resume/project narratives
- frontend, backend, DevOps, security, or management deep dives
- resume and project explanation practice
- mock interview with scoring and feedback

### 3. Run a Session

- Give one exercise or question at a time.
- Let the user answer before judging when doing mock practice.
- Evaluate correctness, communication, tradeoffs, edge cases, and time management.
- Give a better model answer only after feedback.

### 4. Improve

- Identify patterns in mistakes.
- Assign focused drills and revision topics.
- Track readiness by interview type.
- Convert weak areas into the next focused session.

## Rules

- Do not overwhelm the user with a giant study plan unless asked.
- Prefer realistic interview constraints.
- Be direct about gaps and specific about how to fix them.
- Do not claim insider knowledge of a company's current interview loop unless verified from current sources.
- Do not run three interview types at once unless the user asks for a broad plan.
